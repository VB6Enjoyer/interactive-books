Core Concepts

The logical structure of Rothbard's arguments.

Self-Ownership

Every man has an absolute right to the control and ownership of his own body. This is the axiom from which all else flows.

Homesteading

Unused land becomes property when an individual "mixes their labor" with it. The first user is the rightful owner.

Non-Aggression

No one may initiate the use of physical force against another's person or property. Violence is only valid in self-defense.

The State

Defined as an organization that acquires revenue through coercion (taxation) and claims a monopoly on defense. Rothbard views it as a "criminal gang."

Contract

Contracts are transfers of property titles. A mere promise is not enforceable; only the transfer of alienable property is.

Lifeboat Situations

Extreme emergencies do not invalidate property rights. If you own the boat, you decide who stays. If unowned, first come, first served.
